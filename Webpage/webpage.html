My Programming Journey Emily Rever

Since stumbling into HTML and CSS I have become a hopeful soon to be Web Developer. Since being exposed to programming, I have found it to be a great mix of creativity and problem solving and have enjoyed the challenges it has provided me with. Previously visiting websites, I would have no thought about designs, only focus on if the loading times were too long! Now I find interest in how I would change common websites I visit to improve layouts and user interactivity to provide a better experience. I am excited for my future development as a developer so I can make something incredible like the websites I have bookmarked for great designs. I hope in the future I can use these as inspiration for my own work that I can be proud of.

I am excited and driven to apply for Founder & Coders as I finally found my calling, a subject and future job prospect that I find engaging, motivating and something that I spend my spare time reading about simply to learn more. After looking up the course structure, I realised how beneficial the project based format is for my development as learning is far more intuitive from trying something yourself or as part of a team than just reading it from a resource. Peer to peer learning is exciting as it’s inspirational to learn from someone who is excited to share what they have recently learnt. Solutions to problems are also far more likely to be remembered if you’ve had to investigate them as a group as opposed to just being told how to do it.

My latest interests in coding have been focusing on learning and developing my skills in CSS Grid and Flexbox. I have also been taking Harvard’s CS50 course online with a fellow F&C applicant. Other passions of mine include cooking and sewing. I find both therapeutic and stress relieving processes with both having end goals of feeling proud of what you have accomplished. Another is animals and I look forward to the day when I finally live somewhere I can have some.

I have always had an interest in science and studied Medical Neuroscience at University so enjoy reading about new discoveries, particularly in the Alzheimer and Dementia fields.
